# CS-255 Portfolio

## DriverPass Project Reflection

### Project Summary
The DriverPass project had the goal of designing a web based driver education and scheduling system for DriverPass, which is a company that aids students in preparation for their DMV written and driving tests. The client needed a safe and secure cloud-based solution which would enable customers to register, purchase driving training packages, arrange driving lessons, access on-line learning information, complete practice tests and monitor their progress. Tools for managing customers, instructors, appointments, vehicles, reports and system records were also needed for employees and administrators.

### What I Did Well
One of the strongest parts of this project was gathering and organizing the business requirements. I converted the client's requirements to a set of functional and non-functional requirements which became the requirements for the system design. I also developed UML diagrams of the system such as the use case diagram, activity diagram, sequence diagram, class diagram that accurately captured the processes in the system and relationship between users and system components.

### What I Would Revise
If I had the chance to change something about my work, I would add more security and error handling procedures to the system design. I would include other diagrams or documentation, for instance, to indicate how to recover passwords, how accounts are locked out, how exceptions are handled, how backups and recoveries are done. These additions would fill in the gaps, and make the design ready for implementation in a real world.

### Understanding User Needs
I understood the user's needs after carefully reading and analyzing the DriverPass interview thus identifying the features that were most important to the client. The business requirements were recorded in the business requirements and then converted to the system design using UML diagrams. To ensure that a system is successful in meeting the client's business needs and that it is secure, reliable and easy for customers and employees to use, it is important to consider the needs of the users.

### My Approach to Software Design
I design software by first identifying the client's objectives and seeking out information on the actual requirements. I use requirement analysis, process modelling and UML diagrams as well as object-oriented design concepts to make the system function in my mind before implementing it. In future projects, I will continue to use these techniques, looking for the feedback of stakeholders so that in the end the system will satisfy the needs of both the business and the user.
